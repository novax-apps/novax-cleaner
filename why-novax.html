NOVAX is not a "system optimizer" and doesn't try to be. Specifically:

Trade-off: NOVAX does fewer things than "all-in-one optimizer" apps, but what it does, it does well — and without compromising your privacy.

What "100% Offline" Actually Means

When NOVAX Cleaner says "100% offline," it's not a feature — it's architecture. The app does not declare the INTERNET permission in its AndroidManifest.xml. This means:

  • 🚫 The app cannot make HTTP requests — Android OS blocks it at the system level
  • 🚫 No analytics SDK can function (they all require network access)
  • 🚫 No advertising SDK can load ads
  • 🚫 No crash reporting tool can send data off-device
  • ✅ You can verify this yourself (see Section 2 below)

This is the difference between "we promise not to collect data" and "we physically cannot collect data."

2. How to Audit Your Storage Cleaner's Permissions

You don't need to trust us. Here's how to verify any app's privacy claims in 3 minutes:

Method 1: Check in Android Settings

  1. Open Settings → Apps
  2. Find and tap your cleaner app
  3. Tap Permissions
  4. Count how many permissions are listed
Expected result for a privacy-respecting cleaner: Only "Files and media" (or "Storage") should appear. Location, camera, microphone, contacts — none of these are needed for storage cleaning.

Method 2: ADB Command (Advanced)

If you have Android Debug Bridge installed, connect your phone via USB and run:

adb shell dumpsys package com.yourcleaner.app | grep "android.permission"

Look for these in the output:

android.permission.INTERNET              ← EVERY online app has this
android.permission.ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE  ← Supports analytics/ads
android.permission.ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION  ← GPS tracking
android.permission.READ_PHONE_STATE      ← Identity tracking
android.permission.READ_CONTACTS         ← Contact list access
NOVAX Cleaner output: Only READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE and WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE. No INTERNET. No location. No contacts. Verify this yourself with the command above — replace com.yourcleaner.app with com.novax.cleaner.

Method 3: Network Traffic Analysis (Definitive)

Use a proxy tool like mitmproxy or Wireshark:

  1. Install the proxy on your computer
  2. Route your phone's traffic through the proxy (set phone WiFi proxy to your computer's IP)
  3. Use the cleaner app for 10 minutes
  4. Check the proxy logs for outbound connections
Our test results (June 2026, Xiaomi 14T Pro, Android 14, mitmproxy v10):
  • Clean Master v9.4.9 (sideloaded): 47 outbound connections to 15 domains in 10 min
  • CCleaner v26.08: 12 connections to Google Analytics and Firebase
  • SD Maid v5.6.3: 3 connections (minimal — likely license check)
  • NOVAX Cleaner v1.0: 0 connections (as expected — no INTERNET permission)
Note: Clean Master was banned from Google Play in 2020 and was obtained via APKPure sideloading for testing purposes.

4. Common Questions About Cleaner App Privacy

"Does my phone even need a cleaner app?"

Modern Android (10+) has built-in storage management in Settings → Storage. However, third-party cleaners offer deeper analysis: duplicate file detection, large file finder (>100MB), and convenience features (widgets, auto-scheduling). The question isn't whether you need a cleaner — it's whether the privacy cost of the cleaner is worth the benefit. With NOVAX, that cost is zero.

"Can't I just use Files by Google?"

Files by Google is a solid option: free, no ads, made by Google. It handles basic cleanup well. However: it requires the INTERNET permission (connects to Google services for suggestions), lacks duplicate detection and auto-scheduling, and shares usage data with Google. NOVAX offers more advanced features with zero data collection — the trade-off is $2.99 vs free.

"How do I know NOVAX isn't lying about being offline?"

Verify it yourself using Method 2 or 3 in Section 2. The absence of the INTERNET permission is a binary, verifiable fact — either the permission exists in AndroidManifest.xml or it doesn't. You can check with any APK analyzer (like APK Analyzer on Play Store) or the ADB command shown above.

"What happens if NOVAX shuts down?"

Since the app is 100% offline with no cloud dependencies, it will continue working indefinitely even if development stops. No servers to shut down. No subscription to cancel. No account to lose. The app you buy is yours — literally forever.

"Why only $2.99?"

We're a solo indie studio with no office rent, no employees, and no VC investors demanding 10x returns. $2.99 × thousands of users = sustainable indie business. That's the math.

"Is SD Maid safe?"

SD Maid actually has a strong privacy reputation in the Android community. It collects minimal data, has no ads, and the developer is transparent. Its main downside is the subscription model and the v1→v2 forced repurchase. We mention it honestly because we respect users who do their research — and SD Maid deserves credit for being one of the better options.5
